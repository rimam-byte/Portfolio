Introduction of Neilson’s 10 Usability Heuristics:

Jakob Nielsen’s 10 usability heuristics are a set of guiding principles used in user interface and user experience design to make systems easier, clearer, and more efficient to use. They focus on improving interaction between people and technology by ensuring that systems are consistent, predictable, and user-friendly. For example, the heuristics emphasize visibility of system status, preventing errors, offering clear feedback, and matching digital systems with real-world concepts that users already understand. By following these heuristics, designers can create websites and applications that are not only functional but also intuitive, reducing user frustration and improving overall satisfaction.
